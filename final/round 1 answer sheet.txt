Answer Sheet

1. Which phenomenon describes the splitting of a single particle into two particles, such as electron-positron pairs, in the presence of a strong electric field?
   Answer: D. Pair production
   Explanation: Pair production is a phenomenon where a single photon splits into an electron and a positron in the presence of a strong electric field.

2. Which was the first general-purpose electronic digital computer?
   Answer: A. ENIAC
   Explanation: The ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer) was the first general-purpose electronic digital computer, completed in 1945.

3. Who is considered the father of computer science?
   Answer: A. Alan Turing
   Explanation: Alan Turing is considered the father of computer science for his foundational work in developing theoretical computer science and the concept of algorithms.

4. Which of the following is responsible for mediating the electromagnetic force?
   Answer: C. Photon
   Explanation: The photon is the elementary particle responsible for mediating the electromagnetic force in the framework of quantum field theory.

5. Which country is credited with the invention of paper?
   Answer: C. China
   Explanation: Paper was invented in China during the Han Dynasty, around 105 AD, by Cai Lun.

6. What is the term for the hypothetical region of spacetime with extremely strong gravitational effects from which nothing, not even light, can escape?
   Answer: D. Black hole
   Explanation: A black hole is a region of spacetime with gravitational effects so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it.

7. How many color dots make up one color pixel on a screen?
   Answer: D. 3
   Explanation: One color pixel on a screen is typically made up of three color dots: red, green, and blue (RGB).

8. What was the name of the mission that successfully landed the first humans on the Moon in 1969?
   Answer: D. Apollo 11
   Explanation: Apollo 11 was the mission that successfully landed the first humans, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin, on the Moon on July 20, 1969.

9. Who is credited with the discovery of X-rays in 1895?
   Answer: D. Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen
   Explanation: Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen discovered X-rays in 1895, an achievement for which he was awarded the first Nobel Prize in Physics in 1901.

10. What is the term for the first successful test of the atomic bomb, conducted by the United States in July 1945?
    Answer: C. Trinity test
    Explanation: The Trinity test, conducted on July 16, 1945, was the first successful detonation of an atomic bomb by the United States.

11. Who is the father of internet?
    Answer: B. Vint Cerf
    Explanation: Vint Cerf, along with Bob Kahn, is considered one of the fathers of the Internet for their work on TCP/IP protocols.

12. When was the first public demonstration of electric light by Thomas Edison?
    Answer: B. 1879
    Explanation: Thomas Edison publicly demonstrated his incandescent light bulb for the first time on December 31, 1879.

13. What is the significance of the work of Leonardo da Vinci in mechanical engineering?
    Answer: B. He designed numerous mechanical devices and concepts
    Explanation: Leonardo da Vinci is renowned for his detailed sketches and designs of numerous mechanical devices and concepts, which were ahead of his time.

14. Program designed to perform specific task is known as 
    Answer: B. Application Software
    Explanation: Application software is designed to perform specific tasks for users, such as word processing, web browsing, or gaming.

15. Bit stands for
    Answer: A. Binary digits
    Explanation: A bit, or binary digit, is the smallest unit of data in computing, representing a binary value of 0 or 1.
